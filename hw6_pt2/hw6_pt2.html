Homework 6 Part 2

Map Description

This map shows the number of young adults with less than a high school diploma or equivalent per thousand young adults in each of Hawaii's five counties.

Reason for Ratio

The ratio set was residents aged 18-24 with less than a high school diploma or equivalent divided by the total number of residents aged 18-24 multiplied by 1000. This was chosen as the ratio to help visualize the percent of each county's youth population that lacked a complete high school education in comparison to its total youth population. Total youth population was used to normalize the data as utilizing another category could have resulted in a map that was more unhelpful. And the 1000 multiplier was used to help ground the data in a commonly used, specific number of people.

Equal Count Map

This mode helps to clearly show the order in which the counties rank in terms of their concentration of youth residents without a high school diploma or equivalent. However, it isn't quite as clear about the extent in which these counties range.

Equal Interval Map

This mode gives a good idea of both how the counties rank amongst themselves and how the concentration ranges, with Kauai County having the largest concentration. The flaws aren't quite as easy to see with the small sample size of counties, but it can sometimes result in misleading visualizations when the data is uneven.

Pretty Breaks Map

This mode helps to show the vast difference between the county with a higher concentration and all of the others. However, it also is a little difficult to see the differences between all of the other counties' concentrations.
